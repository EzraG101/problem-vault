\section*{Solution}

\begin{statement}{Cute Inequality}
	Let $a,b,c\ge1$ be real numbers. Show that $a+b+c-abc\le 2$. 
\end{statement}
We have
\[ 0\le (a-1)(b-1)(c-1)+(a-1)(b-1)+(b-1)(c-1)+(c-1)(a-1)=abc-a+b+c+2, \]
from which the inequality follows. $\blacksquare$
